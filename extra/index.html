Practice Your Language Skills

Reading Practice

Read one of 60 original short stories written specifically for language learning. Reading is incredibly helpful.

Speaking (Pronunciation) Practice

Record a given sentence and compare your voice with a native speaker. This allows you to practice your pronunciation.

Listening Practice

Hear a given sentence and place the words in the correct order. This will help you practice your listening skills.

Writing Practice

Hear a given sentence and try to type what you hear, this difficult exercise will help you practice your writing skills.

Memory Practice (Flash Cards)

Review words and sentences you've manually added to your review list through a simple Flash Card interface.

Choose your difficulty level

Level 1: Beginner

Very short stories with simple ideas. Serves as an introduction to reading in a new language. Uses a mix of styles and moods.

Level 2: Intermediate

Longer stories with intermediate vocabulary written in an anecdotal style. This is where it truly starts to be fun! First-person narrative. Mostly present tense.

Level 3: Advanced

Complex stories with advanced vocabulary and syntax written in the style of fiction novels. Third-person narrative. Mostly past tense.

Choose your difficulty level

Level 1: Reading & Hearing

Repeat after the native speaker and compare your pronunciation. You can use the written form of the sentence to help you.

Level 2: Reading Only

Practice from written material by reading the sentence. Try to have the correct pronunciation without hearing it first.

Level 3: Hearing Only

Repeat after the native speaker and compare your pronunciation without the written form. You'll have to listen carefully.

Hear & Write Information

Listen to the sentence in the audio clip carefully and type what you hear. Type the correct amount of words and press enter or click "submit" to have your text analyzed.

Use the sentence below and the small numbered graphic to the left of the larger picture to guide you. Some words may have alternate spellings but you can use the underscores below as guidance.

Words joined by a hyphen (-) are considered two words. You can type the hyphen, but typing a space will not be considered a mistake and won't affect your score.

Words joined with an apostrophe (') are considered single words by the program (even if they are technically two words). Typing the apostrophe is required and will be marked as a mistake if omitted.

Typing the special characters is preferred, but won't be counted as a mistake if omitted. You will however, be notified of the omission (the important special characters will be highlighted in green).
